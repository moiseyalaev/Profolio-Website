Academic and Work Life

Personal Background


  Hi there! As you may have guessed my name is Moisey Alaev. A fun fact about me is that both my first and last name were technically misspelled when I immigrated from Uzbekistan at the age of 5. Originally, my name is Моисей Алаев which translates to Mosses Alayev. I come from a traditional family of Bukharian Jews. Since Uzbekistan was a satellite state of the Soviet Union, my community historically faced religious persecution. Therefore, when Uzbekistan declared independence, my family—along with thousands of other Bukharian families—fled to New York City. Of course, I experienced the 'typical' transitional challenges that immigrant families know all too well. However, growing up in Queens I had the unique experience of being surrounded by a myriad of different cultures and people. This allowed me to understand the importance of giving everyone a chance to tell their story and share their perspective. On the flip side, it made me realize that when it comes down to it we are all just human and we all face our own set of challenges and struggles.

  When it came time to decide on a college I wanted to go somewhere that not only challenged me academically, but would also push me to be a better and more responsible person. Following that, I started UCLA in 2018 as a math of computer science major. This was the second time in my life where I was really challenged to acclimate to my surroundings. Fast-forward over three years later and my girlfriend and I have been living together for over a year and recently adopted the cutest german shepherd/husky mix, Luna. As it stands, I believe that I am ready to take on the next chapter in my life by starting to work full time. I believe that my life experiences have prepared me for anything that comes my way, and I am excited to take it on!
