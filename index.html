Personal Background


  Hi there! As you may have guessed my name is Moisey Alaev. A fun fact about me is that both my first and last name were technically mispelled when I immigrated from Uzbekistan at the age of 5. Originally, my name is Моисей Алаев which translates to Mosses Alayev. I come from from a traditional family of Bukharian Jews. Since Uzbekistan was a satellite state of the Soviet Union, my community historically faced religious persecution. Therefore, when Uzbekistan delcared independence, my family-along with thousand of other Bukharian families-fled to New York City. Of course, I experienced the 'typical' transitional challenges that immigrant families know all too well. However, growing up in Queens I had the unique experience of being surrounded by a myriad of different cultures and people. This allowed me to understand the importance of giving everyone a chance to tell their story and share their perspective. On the flip side, it made me realize that when it comes down to it we are all just human and we all face our own set of challenges and struggles.

  When it came time to decide on a college I wanted to go somewhere that would not only challenege me academically, but would also push me as to be a better and more responsible person. Following that, I started UCLA in 2018 as a math of computer science major. This was the second time in my life where I was really challeged to acclimate to my surroundings. Fast-foward over four years later and my wife, Emily, and I have made a home in Dallas, Texas where we welcomed our first baby, Mila, and out German Sheperad/Husky mix, Luna. I believe these life experiences have prepared me to adapt to any challenges that come my way, motivating me to coninuously improve as a father, husband, and a professional Software Engineer/ Data Scientist.
